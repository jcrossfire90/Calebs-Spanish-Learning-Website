Lesson 1: Spanish Alphabet

The foundation of pronunciation

Why Learn the Alphabet?

Spanish pronunciation is much easier than English. Most letters are pronounced consistently. Once you learn the sounds, reading Spanish becomes much easier.

The Five Vowels

These are the most important sounds in Spanish. Unlike English, they almost always sound the same.

Letter Spanish Sound English Approximation
A ah father
E eh bet
I ee machine
O oh go
U oo rule

*H - The Spanish H sound used in the names of the letters G and J is pronounced deep in the throat, similar to clearing your throat. English does not have this exact sound.

*rr - The Spanish RR is rolled or trilled by vibrating the tongue against the roof of the mouth.

💡 Pronunciation Help

*H - A strong "h" sound made in the back of the throat. It is stronger than the English H.

*rr - A rolled R sound made by quickly vibrating the tongue.

🔊 Pronunciation Tip

English speakers often make these two mistakes:

  • I is pronounced like the English letter E ("ee"), not like "eye".
  • E is pronounced "eh", similar to the vowel sound in bet, not like the English letter E.

Remember:

  • I = ee
  • E = eh

Practice saying:

"E, I, E, I" → "eh, ee, eh, ee"

💡 Spanish Note

In most Spanish-speaking countries, including Mexico, the letters ll and y are pronounced very similarly.

Examples:

  • lluvia ≈ yuvia
  • pollo ≈ poyo

In some countries, such as Argentina and Uruguay, these letters may sound more like "sh."

Practice

Say each vowel out loud:

  • A = ah
  • E = eh
  • I = ee
  • O = oh
  • U = oo

Repeat them slowly and then faster.
